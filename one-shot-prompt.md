CREATIVE DIRECTION BRIEF: KALYAN ELEVATORS
1. BRAND PERSONALITY & DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
Kalyan Elevators is a premium global manufacturer of luxury vertical transportation systems. The brand should be positioned at the intersection of masterful engineering, architectural integration, and timeless elegance.

Brand Personality: Confident, engineered, timeless, architectural, refined, elegant, trustworthy, and innovative.
The Emotional Feeling: Step into a world of quiet power, precision engineering, and luxurious architectural harmony. The feeling should evoke the heavy, satisfying sound of a high-end luxury car door closing or the silent glides of an elite hotel elevator.
Luxury Level: Ultra-premium. The digital experience must feel like a premium luxury brand (similar to Bugatti, Steinway & Sons, or premium architectural studios like Zaha Hadid Architects), moving far away from the basic, generic look of a local contractor.
Pacing & Sophistication: The pages must breathe. High-end design is defined by restrained layouts, deliberate white space, and a measured rhythm that doesn't rush the user. The pacing should feel cinematic, guided by smooth, elegant transitions.
2. COLOR SYSTEM
The color palette represents high-end metals, architecture, and luxury finishes. Use a high-contrast dark-mode-first aesthetic with warm, premium accents:

Primary Backgrounds: Slate-charcoal (#121212 or #161616) for dark immersive sections; warm bone-white (#F9F6F0) or soft linen (#F4F4F4) for light sections to establish a premium editorial feel. Avoid harsh solid blacks and stark paper whites.
Accent Color: Antique Brushed Brass / Warm Metallic Gold (#c8922c to #D4AF37). Use this sparingly for key brand elements, highlighting headers, primary buttons, and icons.
Neutrals: Muted silver-grey (#8E8E93 or #C7C7CC) for subtitles, and deep charcoal (#1F1F1F) for borders and text selections.
Contrast Strategy: Alternate between deep, dark-mode cinematic sections (Hero, Stats, Product Range Banners) and airy, high-contrast light sections (Core Features, Success Stories, Client Reviews) to create a dynamic visual cadence while scrolling.
3. TYPOGRAPHY DIRECTION
The typography must feel editorial, architectural, and highly structural. Move away from generic sans-serif fonts to achieve an Awwwards-worthy standard:

Headline Typeface (Refined & Expressive): Pair an elegant, high-contrast display serif or geometric sans-serif (e.g., Syne, PP Neue Montreal, Cormorant Garamond, or Ogg) for the primary headings. Headings should have generous letter-spacing (tracking-tight or tracking-wide depending on character widths) and use varying font weights (e.g., pairing a bold serif with a light italicized serif) to create high-end fashion/architecture layout styles.
Body Typeface (Clean & Engineered): Use a highly legible, low-contrast geometric sans-serif (e.g., Outfit, Inter, or Satoshi) for body copy and metadata. Keep body text sizes restrained (14px–16px) with comfortable line-heights (1.6x) to preserve generous white space.
Metadata & Accents: Small, all-caps monospace labels or geometric sans-serif sub-labels with wide letter spacing (e.g., letter-spacing: 0.15em) for stats, card categories, and small tags.
4. VISUAL LANGUAGE & PREMIUM EFFECTS
Elevate the visual styling from a basic grid to a layered, high-fidelity experience using the following guidelines:

Architectural Grid Overlay: Implement a subtle background grid system with extremely thin, low-opacity borders (e.g., rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.05) on dark backgrounds, or rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.05) on light). The layouts should align perfectly to these grid lines, mimicking architectural blueprints.
Card Design: Cards should not have default box shadows or heavy solid borders. Instead, use thin borders (1px) in brushed metal or low-opacity grey, subtle glassmorphism (backdrop-filter: blur(12px)), and slight inner glow highlights. Give them a modern, refined corner radius (typically 8px to 12px—avoid overly rounded pills for structural elements, keeping them crisp and clean).
Interactive Buttons:
Primary CTA: A custom-designed button with a sleek hover animation (e.g., a liquid-fill background slide, or a shifting chevron that breaks past the button border).
Call Buttons: Floating or inline action buttons in warm gold with clean SVGs rather than default phone icons.
Shadows & Lighting: Use layered, soft ambient shadows (diffused and high-blur) to create depth. Introduce subtle metallic gradient highlights on card edges or backgrounds to evoke the look of polished steel and brass elevator cabins.
5. PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION
The website relies heavily on cinematic imagery. Instruct the visual assets to feel like a high-end architectural digest rather than stock catalog photos:

Composition & Framing: Wide-angle architectural shots showing elevators integrated seamlessly into their environment (e.g., modern luxury residential lobbies, minimalist glass elevator shafts inside multi-level modern concrete villas).
Lighting: Dramatic natural light, soft golden-hour glows, sharp geometric shadows, and reflections on polished metals and glass panes.
Editing Style: Sophisticated, slightly desaturated color grading with boosted contrast in industrial settings, emphasizing texture (brushed steel, wood grains, gold leaf trims, concrete, marble).
People in Imagery: Model-like, elegant subjects (e.g., business professionals, modern luxury homeowners) interacting naturally and calmly, emphasizing lifestyle, comfort, and premium living rather than forced stock smiles.
6. MOTION & INTERACTION LANGUAGE
The movement throughout the site must feel fluid, silent, and highly controlled, mimicking a perfectly calibrated elevator ride.

Transitions & Reveals: Use smooth split-text reveal animations for headings on scroll, where lines slide up from behind mask containers.
Image Reveals: Implement a smooth "curtain" slide or scale-down reveal on scroll for large project images (e.g., starting at scale 1.05 and easing to 1.0 on enter).
Hover States: When hovering over interactive cards, apply subtle, organic micro-interactions:
Card backgrounds slightly shift in gradient angle or blur intensity.
Images zoom in by 2% with an easing curve (cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)).
Icon shapes (like the gold plus icons in the benefits grids) rotate smoothly or morph into minimalist arrow pointers.
Sticky & Parallax Elements: Implement subtle vertical parallax on large background skyscrapers and elevator shafts. The header navigation bar should transition from fully transparent to a frosted-glass sticky element as the user scrolls past the hero section.
7. USER EXPERIENCE (UX) & CONVERSION STRATEGY
A luxury purchase is built on trust, authority, and safety. The UX path should guide users from inspiration to customization:

Trust Signals: Place internationally recognized safety certifications (TÜV SÜD, CE, ISO) in a clean, high-end logo strip early in the journey. Include prominent, beautifully styled Google Reviews alongside video case studies.
Typographical Quality & Quality Assurance: Ensure the copy is flawless. Correct all errors present in the original reference site, such as:
Change "Client's Say" to "Clients Say" (possessive correction).
Change "UNDER TAKEN PROJECTS" to "UNDERTAKEN PROJECTS".
Ensure proper capitalization of cities like "Bangalore" and "Kochi".
Remove accidental spaces before question marks in FAQ sections.
Refine "Connect Us" buttons to "Connect With Us" or "Book a Consultation".
Correction on Territory: Under "Overseas Regions," re-categorize Lakshadweep (which is an Indian Union Territory) to a domestic island division or update the section name to reflect regional hubs.
Interactive Forms: Form fields should not use basic input boxes. Instead, use clean bottom-border lines that highlight in gold upon focus, with floating labels that lift elegantly.

B. THE 10 SPECIFIC PRODUCT PAGES
Each of the following elevator categories must have its own dedicated product page structured around technical excellence, architectural integration, and specific use-cases:

Passenger Elevators: Focus on residential towers and high-rise apartments. Emphasize ride comfort, noise reduction, and modern cabin aesthetics.
Home Elevators: Focus on villas, penthouses, and private estates. Highlight ultra-quiet operation, small footprint, gearless motors, customizable interior paneling (wood veneers, brass mirrors), and absence of a traditional machine room (MRL).
Glass Elevators: Focus on natural light and open-concept architecture. Emphasize panoramic glass panels, sleek steel/brass frames, and visual integration into modern building voids.
Capsule Elevators: Focus on external building pods, hotels, and luxury shopping centers. Highlight the iconic curved glass capsule shapes, exterior architectural impact, and scenic passenger experience.
Commercial Elevators: Focus on heavy foot traffic, corporate lobbies, and office complexes. Detail smart dispatching systems, destination control panels, high travel speed, and large group configurations.
Hospital Elevators: Focus on medical logistics. Emphasize smooth start/stop curves, stretcher accessibility, hygiene-compliant stainless steel walls, priority override buttons for emergency runs, and wide doors.
Car Elevators: Focus on luxury showrooms and residential garages. Highlight load capacity, dual control panels (accessible from the car window), structural safety floor locks, and space-saving vertical parking tech.
Freight Elevators: Focus on heavy cargo transport. Detail industrial construction, reinforced steel floors, manual or vertical bi-parting doors, and rugged durability metrics.
Goods Elevators: Focus on logistics, warehouses, and retail spaces. Detail practical mechanical design, reliability metrics, and ease of loading.
Structural Elevators: Focus on self-supporting structural shafts, outdoor lift frames, and challenging installations where a traditional concrete shaft does not exist. Highlight self-supporting glass/steel towers and weatherproofing.
