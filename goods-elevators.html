FAQ

Questions,
answered.

Goods lifts serve lighter, high-frequency duties — stock trolleys, cages and parcels — while freight lifts are built for heavy industrial loads and vehicle entry.

Compact 250–500 kg cars fit within a standard storeroom footprint; larger 1,000–2,500 kg cars handle palletised deliveries.

Attended-goods configurations are certified for accompanied travel; goods-only units are interlocked to prevent passenger use.

Self-supporting shaft options and phased works keep most retail installations to a few weeks, coordinated around trading hours.

Preventive visits on a fixed calendar plus remote monitoring — most issues are resolved before the stockroom ever notices.
